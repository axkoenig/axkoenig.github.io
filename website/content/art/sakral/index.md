This series explores sacred architecture and its unique relationship with light, space, and form. The photographs capture the ethereal quality of these religious structures, revealing how architecture can transcend its materiality to create spaces of contemplation and transcendence.

![Sakral 4](media/sakral/4.jpeg)

Kópavogskirkja, Kópavogur

Santuario Madonna delle Lacrime, Syracuse  
Michel Andrault and Pierre Parat, 1994

The work examines how these structures use form, light, and material to create spaces that facilitate spiritual experience, documenting the intersection of architecture and faith.
