AUFNAHME 01 August 2020

My first vinyl recording opens with warm and heavy sounds by Burial – one of my all-time favourite artists. With "Hall of Mirrors" we stay in the UK and dive into the early 1990s which was an evolutionary era for IDM. B12's Electro-Soma in particular, marks one of the foundations of contemporary electronic music in the UK. Tagwell Woods' self titled track introduces us to darker yet peaceful sounds.

The record "The Last Resort" by Danish artist Trentemøller with its rhythmic track "Nightwalker" played an important role when I first got into electronic music. With recent music from the Dutch label De Lichting and the Munich based labels Ilian Tape and SNC RECS we slowly drift into more hectic soundscapes.

In the track "Everything We Call Real" the beautiful poem "The Laughing Heart" by Charles Bukowski is recited by the gentle voice of Tom O'Bedlam. The poem reminds us that our life is our life and that chances will come our way. With "Back to Belgrade" which was originally written as the film score of "The Black Panthers" we fast forward in the Warp Records timeline for 24 years. The distant and emotional sounds of Moby’s "Lilly" mark the end of this recording.
