I am an artist and researcher based in Berlin. I’m currently pursing my PhD in Oliver Brock’s Robotics and Biology Laboratory at TU Berlin. My research focuses on the algorithmic foundations of embodied artificial intelligence. I use dextrous robotic manipulation with soft robotic hands as my research platform, to gain insights into the role of embodiment in learning systems.

Previously, I worked at Merantix Momentum as a Machine Learning Researcher, where I mainly work on self-supervised approaches for computer vision. I completed my master's in Robotics, Cognition, Intelligence at the Technical University of Munich (TUM). During my studies, I was a Research Fellow in the Harvard Biorobotics Lab and the Mechatronics in Medicine Lab at Imperial College London. I hold a B.Sc. in Engineering Science, an interdisciplinary engineering program from TUM.

I am involved in various art projects. For example, I am one half of Aqua Veen. Aqua Veen is a DJ and music production duo from Berlin. In our DJ sets we often blend warm and driving house with more scientific soundscapes. We are about to release our first EP, called “Bleedig EP” which features some ambient, experimental and vertical sounds. I’m also an avid photographer, coming mostly from architectural photography, now moving to more human and intimate photography.

Why am I doing what I am doing? While machine learning and robotics certainly have their merits and will benefit society in some way, my goal is not to replace humans with general purpose robots. Much rather, I am interested in the technical challenges of autonomy in the real world, and from these insight we can learn a great deal about us. We’re going full circle.
